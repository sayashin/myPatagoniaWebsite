Regional Events Calendar

January

  1. National Horse and Tradition Festival. Ingeniero Luiggi, La Pampa.
  2. National Festival of Wheat and Bread. Eduardo Castex, La Pampa.
  3. Regional Leather Roast Festival. Aluminé, Neuquén.
  4. National Pear Festival. Allen, Rio Negro.
  5. National Pear Festival. Allen, Rio Negro.
  6. National Fine Fruit Festival. El Hoyo, Chubut.
  7. National Cherry Festival. The Ancients, Santa Cruz.

February

  1. National Livestock Festival of the Western Pampas. Victorica, La Pampa.
  2. National Festival of the Blue Gulf. The Grottoes, Río Negro.
  3. National Apple Festival. General Roca, Río Negro.
  4. National Festival of the Poster. Junín de los Andes, Neuquén
  5. National Garden Festival. Villa La Angostura, Neuquén
  6. National Confluence Festival. Neuquén, Neuquén.
  7. National Festival of Aguas Blancas. Aluminé, Neuquén.
  8. Roast Festival. Cholila, Chubut.
  9. National Steam Train Festival. El Maitén, Chubut.
  10. National Festival of Lake Argentino. El Calafate, Santa Cruz.
  11. National Festival “Cave of the Hands”. Perito Moreno, Santa Cruz.

March

  1. National Salmon Festival. Shrimp, Chubut.
  2. National Trekking Festival. El Chalten, Santa Cruz.
  3. Valley Agricultural Show. Gaiman, Chubut.
  4. National Sheepdog Festival, Río Grande, Tierra del Fuego.

April

  1. Malvinas Vigil, Río Grande, Tierra del Fuego.
  2. National Festival of Pehuén. Aluminé, Neuquén.
  3. National Chocolate Festival. Bariloche, Río Negro.
  4. Via Crucis Submarine. Puerto Madryn, Chubut.

May

  1. National Gold and Silver Festival. Puerto San Julián, Santa Cruz.

June

  1. National Holiday of the Longest Night. Ushuaia, Tierra del Fuego.
  2. National Festival of Matra and Crafts. Valcheta, Río Negro.

July

  1. Madryn to the Plate. Puerto Madryn, Chubut.
  2. Landing Festival. Gaiman, Chubut.

August

  1. National Snow Festival. Bariloche, Río Negro.
  2. National Snow Sculpture Festival. Ushuaia, Tierra del Fuego.

September

  1. Provincial Student Festival and election of the Provincial Queen. Luis Beltrán and Allen, Río Negro.
  2. National Creole Grill Festival. Miguel Riglos, La Pampa.

October

  1. National Festival of the Rider and the Horse. Luis Beltrán, Río Negro.

November

  1. National Chivito Festival, dance and song. Chos Malal, Neuquén.
  2. National Lamb Festival. Puerto Madryn, Chubut.

December

  1. National Cordilleran Christmas Festival. San Martín de los Andes, Neuquén.
  2. National Petroleum Festival. Comodoro Rivadavia, Chubut.
  3. National Coal Festival. Turbio River, Santa Cruz.
  4. Yellow Plume Festival, Puerto Deseado, Santa Cruz.

WHERE TO STAY

A Cabin in the Woods

With a typical architecture of the area, and framed in its landscapes, the cabins are one of the most requested accommodations for those traveling to Patagonia, especially families or groups.
To guarantee a good travel experience, we always recommend hiring accommodation and tourist services that have the corresponding authorization.
The available options for accommodation are: Hotels, Cabins, Apart Hotels, Hostels, Apartments, Private Houses, Dormis and Campings.

TRANSPORTATION

How to Get Around Patagonia

Renting a car for a road trip in Patagonia is known for its high cost. While there are a few small airports in the region, flights tend to be infrequent and pricey. However, the positive aspect is that bus travel in Patagonia is typically quite comfortable, the buses were all roomy and provided a comfortable experience.
