MOUNT FITZ ROY

Fantastic Hikings

Throughout the entire Mount Fitz Roy trek, you'll be treated to breathtaking landscapes. You'll encounter expansive river valley vistas, majestic mountain panoramas, serene forests, and glistening lakes. The pinnacle of this hike is the awe-inspiring sight of the glacier-clad Cerro Fitz Roy, one of Patagonia's most iconic peaks, towering over the turquoise Laguna de los Tres below.

TORRES DEL PAINE

A Backpacking Adventure

This expansive national park is renowned for its breathtaking glaciers, picturesque lakes, verdant forests, and the iconic trio of granite peaks known as the Paine Massif. Whether you opt for a single-day hike or embark on an extended adventure along the W and O Treks, you'll have the opportunity to fully immerse yourself in the splendor of this remote natural marvel. The prime time to visit is from September to May, with December to February being particularly appealing due to the abundance of daylight, with up to 17 hours of daylight during these months.

LOS ALERCES NATIONAL PARK

A Walk Among Ancient Trees

An exceptionally stunning national park, recognized as a UNESCO World Natural Heritage Site in 2017, boasts turquoise lakes, winding rivers, majestic glaciers, cascading waterfalls, and the ancient Alerzal Milenario. This park is a part of the Valdivian forest and is home to larch trees that have thrived for over 2500 years.

SAN CARLOS DE BARILOCHE

Patagonia's Most Famous Town

Bariloche is a city enveloped by ancient woodlands, snow-clad mountains, and pristine, transparent lakes—a quintessential embodiment of Patagonia. This urban center is a hub of excellence nestled amidst some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in the country.

CAVIAHUE / COPAHUE

Take a Healing Thermal Bath

It is located at the foot of Copahue volcano, and on the banks of a large water mirror formed by Caviahue lake. This town, along with Locopué and Las Lajas, constitutes the tourist area called Thermal Patagonia. Copahue thermal springs are worldly renowned for the quality and diversity of their waters.

PERITO MORENO GLACIER

The Eighth Wonder of the World

The Los Glaciares National Park is a region of extraordinary natural splendor, characterized by imposing, lofty mountains and numerous glacial lakes. Among them is Lake Argentino, stretching over 160 km in length. At its remote terminus, three glaciers converge to release their debris into the hazy, grey glacial waters, sending colossal icebergs resembling igloos plunging into the lake with resounding splashes.

PUERTO MADRYN

Walk With Penguins, Touch The Whales

An entrance to encounters with whales, penguins, the Valdes Peninsula, and Welsh customs. Upon the initial glimpse, Puerto Madryn captivates travelers. It stands as one of Patagonia's liveliest cities, featuring a picturesque coastal boulevard that offers panoramic views of the expansive natural amphitheater of Nuevo Gulf.

PUERTO RIO TRANQUILO

Watch Unusual Salvador Dali-esque Caves

The primary allure of Puerto Río Tranquilo lies in its enchanting marble caves. These formations seem as though they've melded into the water, propped up by columns frozen in a moment like lava streams vanishing into the submerged depths of the caves. When the sun shines, its rays bounce off the cave walls and the relatively shallow pools within the caves, casting surreal, undulating patterns across the walls, while the water itself mirrors a kaleidoscope of turquoise hues.

USHUAIA

Be at The End Of The World

Situated at the southernmost tip of Argentina, Ushuaia marks the conclusion of southern Patagonia. It is enveloped by both the sea and the mountain range, offering breathtaking vistas wherever you look, with nature's vibrant colors and vastness never ceasing to amaze. The city provides access to rugged and untamed terrain, with plenty of opportunities for exploration. Moreover, there are numerous day hikes to experience within the Tierra del Fuego National Park.

CERRO TORRE

The Most Beautiful Mountain on Earth

By residing in the neighboring town of El Chaltén, you can access various day hikes offering impressive views of Cerro Torre. However, for a more demanding adventure and a unique perspective on the mountain, there's a challenging route that leads you onto the Ice Cap, allowing you to approach the mountain closely and witness it from a distinct angle as part of an arduous expedition.
