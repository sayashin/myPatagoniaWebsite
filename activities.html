Adventure Tourism

1 / 6

Rafting and Tubing

Explore the thrill of rafting in Patagonia's premier whitewater rivers with six remarkable rafting expeditions available in 2023. Craft your personalized itinerary, allowing you to explore renowned destinations like Balmaceda, Puerto Rio Tranquilo, Mirador Glaciar Exploradores, Patagonia, Baker River, and Punta Arenas.

2 / 6

Mountaineering in Patagonia

Patagonia is renowned for featuring some of South America's most thrilling terrains for mountaineering, with its rugged Andean peaks and the isolated stretches of the Patagonian Ice Cap having drawn aspiring mountaineers for more than a century.

3 / 6

Scuba and Snorkeling in Patagonia

Patagonia offers opportunities for scuba diving in the Atlantic waters, including the Beagle Channel, often referred to as the 'End of the World,' as well as the serene depths of Nahuel Huapi in the Andean lake region.

4 / 6

Horseback Riding

Patagonia offers a range of horseback riding experiences suitable for visitors of every skill level who seek the thrill of riding across the Andes or through the expansive steppes. Torres del Paine, Bariloche, the Lake District, El Calafate, El Chalten, and Aysen stand out as prime regions for horseback riding in Patagonia. You can opt for either day rides or extended horseback adventures, which can span from three to fourteen days in duration.

5 / 6

Parasailing and Paragliding

Cross-country aviators have soared beyond altitudes of 4,000 meters, traversing a rugged and untamed terrain comprising glaciers, mountains, lakes, and turbulent whitewater rivers. Embarking on a cross-country flight from this region also brings the possibility of encountering wildlife, as it serves as the native habitat of the Andean condor. While a chance encounter with one of these majestic birds isn't assured, you're certainly in the ideal location for such an experience.

6 / 6

Kayaking and Canoeing

Connected by immense glaciers and pristine lakes, the unspoiled and wild expanse of Patagonia offers a haven for nature enthusiasts. Additionally, in the realm of aquatic pursuits, Patagonia stands out as a premier destination. Whether you find yourself in Bariloche or the Beagle Channel, water sports aficionados can navigate exhilarating rapids, serene lakes, and glide by imposing glaciers.

Sports

1 / 6

Fly Fhishing in Patagonia

Patagonia provides fly fishermen with access to world-class angling opportunities amidst a stunning array of landscapes. The waters span from serene lakes and gentle streams to wild whitewater rivers. While Patagonia is renowned for its Brown Trout population, anglers can also pursue Salmon, Carp, and the indigenous Perca. Typically, the fishing season extends from mid-November to mid-April.

2 / 6

Skiing and Snowboarding

Some ski resorts will surprise you with their views from the heights of the slopes and the quality of the snow. Some high-level European teams come to train in these latitudes during the southern winter.

3 / 6

Mountain Biking

Explore the ever-changing scenery of Patagonia at a pace that allows you to fully immerse yourself in its beauty. Traverse gravel roads, race along singletrack trails, and quietly approach the region's wildlife, all while being surrounded by the majestic presence of peaks, glaciers, and volcanoes.

4 / 6

Paintball in Ushuaia

Paintball is a strategic game gaining growing recognition as a sport, and Ushuaia's breathtaking landscape provides an ideal backdrop for engaging in this activity.

5 / 6

Ice Skating and Hockey

El Calafate hosts the Ice Festival for three consecutive weekends in July and August. This unique event takes place in the Redonda Bay, which boasts the largest natural ice skating area in South America, spanning approximately 4 kilometers in length, 3 kilometers in width, and with depths ranging from 2 to 40 meters in its shallowest areas.

6 / 6

Golf in Patagonia

Among the various activities that can be carried out on Patagonian soil, the practice of golf is one of the most strongly developed in recent times. Patagonia presents great diversity, made up of magnificent settings for the development of this sport. The enjoyment of physical exercise is combined with the delight of nature in all its abundance and variety.
